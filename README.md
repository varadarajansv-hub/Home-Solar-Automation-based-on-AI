## Brief Scenario:
This is an electrical energy saving project for a typical home in India, using AI methods. Rooftop solar generation with storage battery along with recommended (or mandated) use of solar thermal water heaters and thermal solar cookers together with the use of induction based cooktops replacing coil based heaters are taken for analysis. For residential units, the DISCOM applies a two month billing cycle. When the units consumed exceeds 500 units (for two months), there is a steep esaclation in the pricing. Bidirectional net metering is provided however, the home owner has to pay for the net enery drawn (imported energy minus the exported energy) Any excess is credited at rates substantially lower than the retail rated paid to the DISCOM. The provision of battery helps to maximise self-consumption, avoid billing in the night (battery can supply at night times) and lower slab rates by staying withing 500 unit bar and most essnetially protection is obtained from unexpected DISCOM outages and load shedding and shutdowns for maintenance etc. In future DISCOM will resort to Time-of-Day (ToD) metering when it is able to provide smart energy meters for homes. At that point of time, the use of thermal heating for water and cooking will help lower the home energy demand besides the fact that for all seasons and conditons,thermal heating helps to shave of  critical portions of the home energy demand. This also preserves the battery storage which can be used to mitigate the expensive peak hour pricing in the mornings and evenings. Vagaries of weather such as cloud covers, monsoon seasons and skewed angle during winte, etc, cause a reduction in solar power output. 
## An attempt at the Solution
In this scenario, an  attempt is made to develop an AI model, which will optimise the solar power generation and usage for the home user which shall work for all seasons.
